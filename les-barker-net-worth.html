Les Barker Full Overview and Wiki

Les Barker, a name synonymous with laughter and lyrical genius in the English folk scene, embarked on a journey that carved a niche in the hearts of many.

Born on a chilly day in Manchester, 1947, Barker’s early foray into accountancy was short-lived as he soon discovered his true calling: weaving words into laughter and song.

The Early Days

Barker’s career began in the folk clubs of Britain, a solo performer whose sharp wit and poetic prowess quickly garnered attention. However, it was with the formation of The Mrs Ackroyd Band, named affectionately after his mongrel dog, that Les found a unique avenue for his talents.

The band, featuring classically trained vocalists and a keyboard player, brought Barker’s parodies and poems to life, touring across Britain and beyond.

A Legacy of Laughter and Thought

He was not just a comedian; he was a poet with the rare ability to oscillate between the profoundly serious and the hilariously absurd.

With 77 books and 20 albums to his name, Barker’s work spans a vast array of themes, from the comedic misadventures of Jason and the Arguments to the touching Guide Cats for the Blind, a poem that inspired a series of fundraising CDs for the British Computer Association of the Blind, raising over £40,000.

The Cultural Bard

Barker’s influence extended beyond the folk clubs and into the fabric of British culture.

His knack for parody saw him successfully incorporate the tongue-twisting Welsh town name Llanfairpwllgwyngyllgogerychwyrndrobwllllantisiliogogogoch into a song, a feat that mirrored his playful yet respectful engagement with language and culture.

His efforts to learn Welsh and produce poetry in the language earned him the NIACE Inspire Award as Welsh Learner of the Year in 2008, highlighting his commitment to cultural immersion and understanding.

The Final Curtain

Despite a heart attack in 2008 and a battle with cancer that led to his retirement announcement in October 2022, Barker’s spirit remained unbroken.

He continued to engage with his audience through online platforms until his passing in January 2023.

Barker’s legacy is not just in the laughter he induced or the tears he sometimes drew; it’s in the indelible mark he left on the folk music scene and the literary world.

FAQs about Les Barker

Who was Les Barker?

He was an English poet known for his comedic poetry and parodies of popular songs. He originally came from Manchester but became a fluent Welsh speaker. He began his career as an accountant before becoming a professional poet and performer.

What is Guide Cats for the Blind?

Guide Cats for the Blind is a poem by Barker that became the title track of a fundraising CD series for the British Computer Association of the Blind (BCAB).

The series, comprising five albums, featured performances of Barker’s poems by folk artists and celebrities, and it helped raise significant funds for BCAB.

How many books did Les Barker write?

He authored 77 books, many of which were sold at his performances. His works include a wide range of humorous and serious poems.

What are some notable works by him?

Some of Barker’s most famous works include The Shipping Forecast, Jason and the Arguments, Cosmo The Fairly Accurate Knife Thrower, and Captain Indecisive.

What was Barker’s style in poetry?

Barker’s poetry was known for its humor, clever rhymes, and the ability to captivate audiences with gentle humor. He created recurring characters and themes in his works.

Did Les Barker perform with a band?

Yes, h performeed with The Mrs Ackroyd Band, named after his mongrel dog, Mrs. Ackroyd.

What were his interests outside of poetry?

He was an avid football fan, a fell runner, and enjoyed playing football and running marathons in his younger days. He also loved real and model trains, nature, and photography.

What was Barker’s personal life like?

He never married and was known for being kind and self-effacing. He expressed his views on politics and war through his poetry, and was remembered as a beautiful, eccentric soul with many friends and fans worldwide.

When and how did Les Barker pass away?
