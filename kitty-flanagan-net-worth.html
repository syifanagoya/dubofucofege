Kitty Flanagan Overview and Wiki

Early life and Education

Kitty Flanagan‘s early life laid the foundation for her eventual success in the world of comedy. Born in 1968 in Sydney, Australia, she grew up in a creatively inclined family.

Her father, John Flanagan, is a well-known Australian author, best known for the Ranger’s Apprentice and Brotherband novel series. This creative environment undoubtedly played a role in shaping her artistic inclinations.

Kitty attended Monte Sant’Angelo Mercy College in North Sydney, where her flair for performance first became evident.

Her interest in comedy and acting was sparked during her school years, particularly when she participated in a school musical production of Alice In Wonderland.

In this production, she humorously portrayed a bunny that pretended to fall asleep, an early indication of her comedic talent.

Early Beginnings and Career Launch

Kitty Flanagan’s journey into comedy began in an unconventional manner. After attending Monte Sant’Angelo Mercy College in North Sydney, she ventured into various jobs, including a stint as a physical education teacher and a copywriter for an advertising agency.

Her foray into comedy was almost serendipitous, starting with open mic nights at a hotel where she worked as a bartender. Her first stand-up performance at the Harold Park Hotel in Sydney was a success, leading her to pursue comedy full-time.

Rise to Fame in Australia

Kitty’s comedic talent was quickly recognized, and she joined the cast of the Australian TV series Full Frontal in 1995 as both a writer and performer. This role significantly boosted her profile in the Australian comedy scene.

She continued to write and act for television, contributing to shows like Shaun Micallef’s World Around Him and The Micallef Program.

International Acclaim and UK Career

In 2001, Flanagan moved to the UK to expand her comedy career. She performed on The Sketch Show and made numerous stand-up appearances, including on The World Stands Up for Comedy Central.

Her time in the UK also saw her writing for various shows on the BBC, Channel Four, and Sky Television.

She regularly returned to Australia for short visits, appearing on shows like Rove, Good News Week, and Spicks and Specks.

Return to Australia and Continued Success

Kitty returned to Australia in 2009, where she continued to thrive in the comedy scene. She joined The Project for segments on trending news topics and began touring Australia with her stand-up comedy show Charming and Alarming.

She also toured with Hello Kitty Flanagan and was involved in the TV comedy series Utopia, playing the role of Rhonda, the public relations manager.

Contributions to the Arts and Advocacy

Kitty has been an advocate for writing and writers in the screen industry. She sponsored the Best Narrative Award at the Heart of Gold International Short Film Festival and was a judge for film scripts.

In 2017, she contributed as a writer for the TV series Drop Dead Weird and began a national tour of her fourth stand-up show, Smashing.

Recent Achievements

In 2021, Flanagan’s sitcom Fisk, which she co-created, co-wrote, co-directed, and starred in, aired on ABC TV. The show was nominated for and won two 2021 AACTA Awards, including Best Narrative Comedy Series and Best Comedy Performer for Kitty Flanagan.

She has also been involved in corporate work and literary events, tailoring her comedy material for various events and participating in literary festivals and charity work.
